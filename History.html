Romulus and Remus

The Story of Romulus and Remus is a weird but very interesting and significant part of Roman history. The story starts off with twins being born and abandoned by their mother. They were found and nursed by a wolf and had later gone on to be raised by a shepard and after all this time had overthrown a tyrant unfortunately the twins in their rivalry had fought and Romulus had killed his brother Remus, becoming the sole founder of the city.

Gladiator

Gladiators where a big piece of history in Rome, these warriors where people who would fight in the now known colosseum. Fighting against other gladiators, slaves, prisoners and animals. They were very strong combatants who carried around weaponry such as a short sword, spears, and shields for defense and offensive attacks. Gladiators were not treated well and were often looked down upon besides entertainment purposes. They were very often slaves used to fight for the people and possibly freedom or prisoners forced into the colosseum. Unfortunately gladiators were very often killed in battle, bled out, or couldn't take the suffering anymore and commited suicide.

Colosseum

The Colosseum is a major part in Roman History. This project was started by the Emperor Vespasian. He'd pass away before this project was completed leaving his two sons in charge to finish the colosseum. Now what was the colosseum used for? It was used for Gladiator fights and showings. These fights could also be prisoners and slaves fighting for there freedom either against gladiators or up against wild animals in a fight to the death where you would be watched by up to 50-80 thousand people wanting bloodshed as this was a big part of entertainment in Rome at the time period.
